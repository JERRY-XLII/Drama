Tanenbaum and MINIX vs Torvalds and LINUX - gentle drama from yesteryear.

8  2012-12-17 by jecniencikn

This is old, from 1992, and people used to use Usenet.

Tanenbaum is a professor and he developed something called Minix. It's a great learning tool. He had a look at the Linux kernel and made some comments.

Torvalds saw those comments, and decided to respond.

*That's one hell of a good excuse for some of the brain-damages of minix. I can only hope (and assume) that Amoeba doesn't suck like minix does. *

Reading the thread it all seems like vigorous debate; nothing too harsh. But in 1992 that was a big thing; Wikipedia says this is sometimes seen as a flamewar. People wrote to Linus to remind him about netiquette.
