Since a couple of years, every October, the hosting company DigitalOcean holds Hacktoberfest (a bad pun in Oktoberfest), which encourages to submit patches to projects hosted in GitHub. The result of having a patch accepted is you get a free Hacktoberfest t-shirt to flex in your geek circle.

Apparently an Indian YouTuber called CodingWithHarry made an small tutorial on how to do pull request, where after cloning [copying] the repo [the code], you make a change, commit [save] it, and ask for the code to be merged upstream. Thing is, his example was basically adding ‚Äúan amazing project‚Äù to a README file: https://twitter.com/joelnet/status/1311773094856454144

What happened? Lots of people, most likely his followers, made accounts and emulated his video. In random repos people made random pull requests: changing readmes, adding dots, ‚Äúfixing‚Äù grammar... low effort shit to get a free shirt. Of course, maintainers were mad because they where being flooded by notifications. A Twatter account was made to track it (pretty funny IMHO): https://twitter.com/shitoberfest

In the end the dude made the video above private and deleted his GitHub account. Now the thing is also opt-in: https://nitter.net/hacktoberfest/status/1312221208667185153
