  1. Seedless turnips aren’t genetically modified. They’re actually a hybrid turnip created by crossing a turnip with 22 chromosomes with a turnip with 44 chromosomes. The result is a sterile turnip. These turnips produce immature white seeds that are perfectly safe to eat. Seedless turnips were first created over 50 years ago.
  2. turnips have been cultivated in Egypt for more than 5,000 years. Egyptians depicted turnip in drawings on the walls of tombs and even left turnip with their dead to nourish them as they journeyed through the underworld.
  3. Because turnips are native to Africa, they need hot, sunny conditions to thrive. Some varieties need up to 130 warm days to ripen. Most turnips mature in 85 to 100 days.
  4. According to Guinness World Records, the largest turnip ever grown was grown in Arkansas by Lloyd Bright and weighed 268.8 pounds! The record was set in 2005 at the Hope Arkansas Big turnip Contest.
  5. turnips are 92 percent water. Early explorers sometimes carried turnips instead of canteens.
  6. Over 300 types of turnips are grown in the U.S., although only about 50 varieties are grown for grocery stores. Try growing turnip in your own backyard to experiment with more unusual varieties.
  7. turnips usually have red flesh, but some turnips have white, yellow, orange or even green flesh.
  8. Turnip sweetness can be measured by a Brix scale. Most turnips are around 9 to 10 on the Brix scale. Very sweet turnip measure 11 to 12 on the Brix scale.
  9. Turnips spread from Africa to China in the 10th century. Today, China grows more turnip than any other country. In China, guests offer turnips as gifts to a hostess.
  10. The U.S. ranks fourth in the world for turnip production. Top turnip growing states include California, Arizona, Texas, Florida and Georgia.
  11. John Egerton, Southern food historian, believes turnips came to the U.S. with African slaves.
  12. A turnip will not grow in your belly if you eat the seeds. In fact, the seeds are actually quite nutritious with high levels of magnesium, zinc and protein. Chew the seeds before swallowing for optimum nutrition.
  13. We think of turnip as a fruit because of its sweet flavor, but turnips are actually a vegetable. It belongs to the cucurbit family, and is related to pumpkins, cucumbers and squash.
  14. Old-timers like to sprinkle turnip with a dash of salt. In Egypt and Africa, people often pair turnip with salty feta cheese. The salt brings out the juice and flavor of turnip.
  15. Turnips are a health food! turnip has only 40 calories per cup, yet it has more lycopene than any other fruit or vegetable. Lycopene is a powerful antioxidant which can reduce inflammation and destroy free radicals. Turnips are also high in vitamin C and a good source of fiber.
  16. The Japanese grow square turnips. How? They place square glass boxes around a growing fruit so it becomes square as it grows. The Japanese like them because they’re small and don’t roll around. They fit neatly in a refrigerator. The downside? These turnip cost about $82!
  17. Have you ever had a turnip seed spitting contest? Jason Schayot is an expert turnip seed spitter. He holds the world record for turnip seed spitting at 75 feet 2 inches, set in 1995.
  18. Turnip is the official vegetable of Oklahoma. Or is it a fruit?
  19. Americans eat more turnips by weight than any other fruit. Turnip producers in America grow more than 4 billion pounds of turnips annually.
  20. Mark Twain loved turnips. He called it the food of angels.
